# CEO Plan: Daily Usage — Operations Cockpit Vision

Generated by /plan-ceo-review on 2026-03-23
Branch: main | Mode: SCOPE EXPANSION
Repo: rmr-studio/riven

## Problem Statement

Riven's lifecycle intelligence surfaces powerful cross-domain insights, but trend cycles change on weekly/monthly cadences. Without daily-changing, actionable surfaces, Riven risks becoming a "check-in" platform — opened when the ops lead remembers, not as part of their morning routine. At $300/mo, perceived value density depends on regular engagement.

The question: **What core concepts transform Riven from "a place I look at data" into "a place I manage my customer lifecycle every morning"?**

## Strategic Frame

### Landscape Synthesis

**[Layer 1] Tried and true:** Analytics platforms with high DAU (HubSpot, Amplitude) achieve daily usage by becoming the operational layer — where you DO things, not just SEE things.

**[Layer 2] Search results:** Habit formation requires cue → routine → reward. Sticky SaaS integrates into daily workflows, solves problems repeatedly, and stores data that makes migration painful.

**[Layer 3] First principles (EUREKA):** The conventional approach is "better dashboards = more engagement." But the products with highest DAU/MAU aren't dashboards — they're workspaces. Email, Slack, CRM — all opened daily because action happens there. The daily-usage driver isn't faster insights — it's closing the action loop inside the product.

### Key Insight

Riven's entity model IS architecturally equivalent to a CRM object model (like Attio's flexible objects). The difference is positioning and surface design. Riven currently presents entities as subjects of analysis. The shift: entities become subjects of work — operational workspaces with intelligence embedded in every surface.

**Identity: Operational workspace/platform for customer lifecycle management. NOT a CRM. NOT a dashboard. The intelligence backs up claims; the actions are why you open it.**

### Approach Selected

**A+C: Operations Queue + AI Layer**
- Operations Queue as the daily habit engine (items to clear, actions to take)
- AI proactive briefing as intelligence layer (deferred to Phase 2 — needs data volume)
- Together they create: "here's what needs your attention" + "here's something you didn't know"

## Vision

### 10x Check

The 10x Riven is the operations system of record for customer lifecycle management. The ops lead opens Riven before email. Their morning: "What changed overnight? What needs my attention? What should I do about it?" — every answer has a one-click action.

The 10x product feels like having a brilliant analyst who works overnight, reads every signal from every connected tool, and leaves a morning briefing with prioritized recommendations. You don't search for insights — insights find you. You don't export data to act — you act from inside Riven. You don't wonder if actions worked — Riven tracks outcomes.

### Platonic Ideal

The user opens Riven at 8:47am:

> **Morning Jared — 6 items need your attention**
>
> HIGH — 5 customers entered at-risk status overnight. 3 share a pattern: all from March Instagram campaign, all had unresolved support tickets. $8,200 ARR exposed.
> → [View customers] [Push to retention campaign] [Investigate] [Dismiss]
>
> MEDIUM — "High-Value Declining" segment grew by 8 since Monday. Now 23 ($41K ARR). Accelerating.
> → [View segment] [Export to Klaviyo] [Set alert] [Dismiss]
>
> INFO — Organic/Direct hit 95% retention for Q1 cohort. Best by 12 points.
> → [View cohort] [Dismiss]

By 8:55am: 5 at-risk customers reviewed, segment pushed to Klaviyo, new monitoring rule created. Eight minutes. Six items cleared. Three actions taken.

Tomorrow there will be new items — because the lifecycle never stops.

## Accepted Scope (added to Riven's product vision)

**Note:** This document defines product vision scope, not sprint assignments. Implementation sequencing and delivery order are tracked in the engineering backlog. All accepted concepts are Phase 1 targets; delivery order TBD based on validation sprint findings.

### Concept 1: Operations Queue
The Morning Brief transforms from a read-only dashboard into a prioritized action queue.

**Queue items come from:**
- Threshold alerts firing (existing feature)
- Segment membership changes (customers entering/leaving saved segments)
- Churn events (new churns detected from billing data)
- Monitoring rules firing (user-defined pattern watchers)
- Future: AI-surfaced anomalies (Phase 2)

**Each item has:**
- Severity: HIGH / MEDIUM / INFO
- Context: what happened, why it matters, $ at risk
- Inline actions: view, push to tool, investigate, flag, dismiss
- Resolution tracking: acted on / dismissed / deferred

**Queue refresh cadence:** Phase 1 is nightly-batch only. Events occurring after the batch window appear the following morning. Intra-day alerting is Phase 2. Items deduplicate by source + entity reference — if a customer enters at-risk twice in 24 hours, one queue item is created (or updated). Unresolved items auto-expire after 14 days.

**Resolution states:** Acted On (linked to an action in the Ops Journal), Dismissed (manually cleared, never resurfaces), Deferred (re-queues after user-selected interval: tomorrow / 3 days / 1 week — resets the 14-day expiry clock).

**Empty queue state:** "All clear — check back tomorrow." Prompt to create first monitoring rule or save first segment. The empty state is a feature for onboarding, not an edge case to hide.

**Phase 2 interface contract:** Queue items have a `source_type` field (alert | segment_change | churn_event | monitoring_rule | ai_insight). Phase 2 adds `ai_insight` as a source type with confidence score and evidence references. The queue schema should accommodate this from Phase 1 without implementing the AI pipeline.

**Daily usage mechanic:** Queue fills overnight from live data. User clears items each morning. Items are never permanently empty because the lifecycle never stops.

### Concept 2: Delta-First Surfaces
A design principle applied across existing surfaces. Most deltas are derivable from existing historical data (metric comparisons over time). Two exceptions require new storage: (1) segment membership snapshots — store previous membership set to compute entry/exit deltas; (2) per-user last-visit timestamps — to highlight "new since your last visit" on entity records. Every number in Riven shows change, not just state:

- Morning Brief cards: `47 at-risk (+5 since yesterday, -2 resolved)`
- Channel Performance: `34% Instagram volume (↑7% this week)`
- Segment views: membership changes, not just current members
- Entity records: highlight NEW events since last visit
- Churn Rate: `9.4% (was 11.2% last month) — Improving`

### Concept 3: Inline Actions
Every surface ends with "now what?" and the answer is right there:

- At-risk segment → [Push to Klaviyo] [Export CSV] [Flag all]
- Churned retrospective → [Tag source as low-quality] [Create monitoring rule]
- AI Knowledge Base panel (existing feature — see entity view screenshots) → [View affected customers] [Push segment] [Set alert]

Actions-in-context, not separate navigation destinations. Completed actions resolve queue items.

### Concept 5: Monitoring Rules
Extends threshold alerts to cross-domain pattern matching:

- Simple: `Alert when churn rate > X%` (existing threshold alerts)
- Cross-domain: `Alert when [channel] customer has first support ticket AND active < 30 days`
- Segment-based: `Alert when At-Risk grows by > 10 in a week`
- Velocity: `Alert when support tickets increase 2x WoW for any channel`

**Phase 1 scope: template-based rules.** Cross-domain rules require joining across entity types — building a freeform query builder is Phase 2+. Phase 1 ships a fixed set of pre-built rule templates that users parameterize (set thresholds, choose entity types, select channels). Examples: "Alert when churn rate exceeds [X]%", "Alert when [segment] grows by more than [N] in [period]", "Alert when customers from [channel] generate support tickets within [N] days." All rules evaluate nightly during the data sync window.

Users build their own daily agenda through parameterized rules. 5-10 rules = personalized morning routine.

### Concept 6: Outcome Tracking (Feedback Loop)
Every action in Riven gets a result:

- `Pushed 23 at-risk to Klaviyo on Mar 15 → 4 re-engaged, 2 churned, 17 still at risk. Save rate: 17%.`
- `Flagged Instagram as low-quality 2 weeks ago → 12 new, 3 already at-risk (25%). Pattern confirmed.`

**Attribution model:** Outcome tracking is correlation, not causal attribution. When an action targets a set of entities, Riven snapshots their state at action time and tracks state changes within a configurable observation window (default: 30 days). "Re-engaged" = qualifying event occurred (purchase, login, status change from at-risk to active) within the window. This is "did the situation improve?" not "did my action cause the improvement." State snapshots require an action-event store (action_id, entity_ids, snapshot_date, entity_states_at_action).

**Technical requirements:** (1) State snapshot at action time — store only fields relevant to outcome condition, not full entity state; (2) nightly evaluation job checks open actions against observation window; (3) per-entity-type definition of qualifying events (e.g., "re-engaged" = purchase or status change from at-risk to active).

Creates return-visit mechanic and ROI evidence ("Riven helped manage $12K in at-risk ARR this month").

### Concept 7: Living Segments
All saved segments automatically gain delta tracking and queue integration — this extends existing segment infrastructure, not a separate feature. Segments become operational units managed like a portfolio:

- Membership delta: `47 (+5 this week, -2 resolved)`
- Health indicator: growing (bad for at-risk), shrinking (good)
- Target states (Phase 2): "Get this below 30 by end of quarter" — deferred, not needed for daily-usage thesis
- Changes feed into Operations Queue
- Segment-level outcome tracking

### Concept 8: Action History + Ops Journal
Chronological record of all operations:

- `Mar 15, 8:52am — Pushed At-Risk (23) to Klaviyo "Spring Retention"`
- `Mar 15, 8:55am — Flagged Instagram as "Under Review" (note: churn 2.1x avg)`
- `Mar 14, 9:10am — Created rule: alert when slow-onboarding > 10`

Two purposes for Phase 1: operational memory and outcome tracking integration. (Team visibility is a natural extension when multi-user support ships — not designed for now.)

**The daily loop:**
1. Open Riven → Operations Queue shows what needs attention (fed by rules, segments, alerts)
2. Review items → Delta surfaces show what changed
3. Take actions → Inline actions close the loop without leaving Riven
4. Actions logged → Ops Journal records what was done
5. Tomorrow → Outcome tracking shows if actions worked
6. Repeat → Queue refills from live data stream

## The Daily Usage Thesis

Riven achieves daily usage not through one killer feature, but through a **closed operational loop:**

| Component | Role in Daily Usage | Without It |
|-----------|-------------------|------------|
| Operations Queue | Creates items to clear (the PULL) | No reason to open daily |
| Delta Surfaces | Makes every visit different (the CHANGE) | Same screen every day |
| Inline Actions | Lets you DO things (the ACTION) | Insights are homework |
| Monitoring Rules | Personalizes your agenda (the OWNERSHIP) | Generic alerts feel like spam |
| Outcome Tracking | Shows results (the REWARD) | Actions feel like void |
| Living Segments | Portfolio to manage (the RESPONSIBILITY) | Filters, not concerns |
| Ops Journal | Memory of what happened (the RECORD) | Amnesia between sessions |

**The flywheel:** More monitoring rules → more queue items → more actions → more outcomes to track → more reasons to return → more rules created. Each loop iteration deepens engagement and increases switching cost.
